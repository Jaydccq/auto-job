# Mode: contacto — LinkedIn outreach helper

1. **Identify targets** with WebSearch:
   - Hiring manager for the team.
   - Assigned recruiter or sourcer.
   - Two to three peers on the team.
   - Interviewer (if a session is already scheduled).

2. **Classify the contact** — ask the user or infer:
   - **Recruiter** — owns talent acquisition or sourcing.
   - **Hiring manager** — leads the team that's hiring.
   - **Peer** — does the same work the user would do (indirect referral path).
   - **Interviewer** — already booked to interview the user on a known date.

3. **Pick one primary target** — the person whose week gets meaningfully
   better if the user joins the team. Everyone else is a backup.

4. **Draft a 3-sentence message** in the contact-type frame below.

   **Recruiter**
   - *Fit*: direct match on role, level, location, availability.
   - *Proof*: one screening-killer fact ("5 years building ML pipelines, NYC,
     available immediately").
   - *Ask*: "Happy to send my CV if this maps to what you're hiring for."

   **Hiring manager**
   - *Hook*: a specific challenge their team is working on (from the JD,
     company blog, or recent talk).
   - *Proof*: the user's most quantitative win that shows the same problem
     pattern solved.
   - *Ask*: "Would love to hear how your team is approaching {challenge}."

   **Peer (referral path)**
   - *Interest*: a real reference to their work — blog post, talk, OSS,
     paper. Not "hey saw you work at X."
   - *Bridge*: something the user is doing in the same space, framed as
     curiosity, not a pitch.
   - *Ask*: "Working on similar problems at {place} — would love your take
     on {topic}."
   - **Do not ask for a referral on the first message.** It happens
     naturally if the conversation continues.

   **Interviewer (pre-meeting)**
   - *Research*: one specific thing about their work or background.
   - *Bridge*: light connection to the user's experience.
   - *Ask*: "Looking forward to our conversation on {date}."
   - **Tone:** prepared, not desperate. Goal is to signal effort, not to
     pitch.

5. **Versions**: English by default. Add a Spanish version only if the
   target's posts/profile are in Spanish.

6. **Backups**: list two alternative targets and one sentence each on why
   they are second-choice.

## Hard rules

- 300-character ceiling (LinkedIn connection request limit).
- No "I'm passionate about", no "results-oriented", no corporate-speak.
- Never share the user's phone number.
- Quote a fact about them, not a pitch about us.
- The contact type changes the **emphasis**; the 3-sentence shape stays
  the same.
